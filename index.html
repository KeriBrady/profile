Welcome!

This website is designed to be a mix of my personal and professional profiles. Have a look around and feel free to contact me via email or on the web!

About me

In every job I’ve held so far – from Quiznos sandwich artist to retail management to social media marketing – I’ve always been the happiest when I’m learning. I also love working with like-minded people and collaborating on new and exciting ideas; the feeling of a huge breakthrough after hours of teamwork is exhilarating and I can’t get enough of it.

Outside of work, I unwind in more solitary ways. Curling up and getting lost in a good book, going for a walk and exploring somewhere new, and catching up with family or friends one-on-one are some of my preferred activities.

I love a lot of things in life… organized and color-coded lists, binging on Netflix, beach days, talking about science, quotes that haunt and stick with me, sketches, planning for the future, deep forest walks, packaging design, new office supplies, catching up with good friends… I could really go on and on.

Professional experience

WWU

I graduated from Western Washington University in 2012 with a Bachelors Degree in Communication and minors in Psychology and Internet Resource Creation and Management. It was here that I discovered my love for communication, especially visual communication, and front-end web development, and was fortunate enough to be able to explore each of those interests.

Retail Management

Throughout college and for almost a year after graduating, I worked in retail management at Hollister Co., then Paper Source. I learned a lot about myself and what I wanted to do in these roles. I thoroughly enjoyed the team aspect of running the stores and helping coworkers do well from my position in management, but wasn’t sure I wanted to move forward in that industry.

Marketing

After Paper Source, I worked in the Marketing department of Smartsheet.com, a tech company located in Bellevue, Washington. Working with a dynamic, driven team was an exciting challenge, and I pushed myself in that role to work on new types of projects with various departments across the company. While I loved the team I was working with, I knew I wanted to be doing a different kind of work – which is why I decided to go back to school in January 2015.

Web UI Dev

I already knew I really enjoyed front-end development work, which is how I ended up at Code Fellows in Seattle. I recently concluded their 4-week Computer Science and Web Development Bootcamp, where I completed multiple projects in HTML, CSS, and Javascript and worked with industry standard development tools. Now with a solid foundation, I’m aiming to move on to the Web UI Accelerator, an 8-week program that goes deep into UI development concepts and will prepare me for my future career.

Future Plans

Once I graduate from Code Fellows, my goal is to land a UI Development job with a company or startup in the Seattle area. My dream job would include a driven, smart, supportive team to work with, the opportunity to learn new skills, a positive and innovative work environment, and the feeling of being eager to go to work and build things every day. It’s a tall order, but one I’m so excited to pursue.
